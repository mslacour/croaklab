Meet the Lab

Dr. Mark LaCour

Mark LaCour is Assistant Professor of Psychology at the University of Louisiana at Lafayette. He earned his Ph.D. in experimental psychology from Texas Tech University with a concentration in cognition and cognitive neuroscience. Before that, he earned an M.S. in psychology and an honors B.A. in philosophy (minor in cognitive science) from the University of Louisiana at Lafayette. He is primarily a cognitive/quantitative psychologist, though he also dabbles in social psychology. His research focuses on judgment, decision-making, and belief processes with a special focus on public health issues (e.g., vaccine hesitancy, risk communication).

Dr. Zebulon Bell

Zebulon Bell is Assistant Professor of Psychology and Coordinator of Undergraduate Research at Louisiana State University of Alexandria (LSUA). He holds a BA in Psychology from Auburn University, an MS in Psychology from Georgia Southern University, and a PhD in Psychology from Oakland University. Both his teaching and research interests sit at the intersection of brain, mind, and behavior. Specifically, his research includes work in spatial and temporal cognition in human and non-human animal species with a special interest in rhythm processing, increasing opportunity for underrepresented youth in STEM disciplines, and improving captive animal welfare and conservation.

Ayush Deshpande

Ayush Deshpande is a graduate of the University of Louisiana at Lafayette, where he earned his B.S. in Psychology (Pre-Medicine Concentration) and B.S.B.A. in Economics, with minors in Chemistry and Competitive Forensics. On the side, he spends time folding origami — he most recently his 'Senbazuru' origami project, where he folded 1000 paper cranes. He is currently working on a project focused on vaccine incentives with Dr. LaCour. Outside of research and hobbies, Ayush works as an Emergency Medical Technician for Acadian Ambulance Service, Inc. in Lafayette, LA.

Chloe Guidry

Chloe Guidry is a graduate student pursuing a M.S. in Psychology at the University of Louisiana at Lafayette. She earned her B.S. in Psychology and her B.A. in Liberal Studies with a concentration in Philosophy from McNeese State University. Her research interests include neurodegenerative diseases, traumatic brain injuries, depression, and psychopathology; her current research utilizes zebrafish as a comparative model and is concerned with the pathogenesis of Alzheimer's disease (AD) as well as how this influences select behaviors in individuals with AD. Outside of graduate school, she works as the senior registrar and Abbreviated Injury Scale specialist for a trauma center and actively participates in the management, analysis, and reporting of data to the National Trauma Data Bank for the purpose of researching (and subsequently reforming) trauma care.
